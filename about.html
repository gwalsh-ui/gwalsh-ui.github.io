My Story

I am currently completing a M.Sc in Interactive Digital Media at the TCD School of Computer Science and Statistics. I graduated with a High 2.1 grade in my Bachelor (Hons) degree of Architectural Science from University College Dublin. I am highly proficient with software such as Photoshop, InDesign, Lightroom, Vectorworks, AutoCAD, Revit and Sketchup.

During my degree, I was awarded a Scholarship to participate in the international programme “Port City, City Limits”, a collaboration between UCD and NCAD, resulting in a temporary exhibition at Ireland’s prestigious Hugh Lane Gallery. In 2017, I was one of five students to be highly commended by the Architectural Association of Ireland in its national student competition. In this competition, I was asked to design a spatial response to facilitate dissent, difference and a shared conversation of “what democracy looks like” responding to the current turbulent political times while addressing the disconnect between the abstraction of college projects and wider reality beyond.

I bring with me a strong design ethos and genuine passion and enthusiasm for technology and design. I thrive under pressure and I am ambitious in my career goals and objectives. I am a dedicated worker, extremely detail oriented and have strong problem-solving skills. I have excellent interpersonal and communication skills and function well as a team member. I am willing, and able, to work long hours to accomplish whatever it takes to meet a deadline.
