Research

Interactive Digital Media Dissertation

The research project investigates the effect increased use of digital platforms during covid-19 is having on virtual well-being, and how design interventions can be used to increase well-being digitally. As we have recently seen the rapid adoption of increased digital communication across all sectors of our economy and social engagements, this research is interested in the mental health impact on its users. This project will carry out a literature review examining the design principles and testing which has been adopted when creating recent projects, delving into any clinical studies as well gaining valuable insights from experts in the field of psychology and computer science on this effect on mental well-being. The research is interested in examining incidences of emotional changes, such as impact digital communication has in either exacerbating or decreasing loneliness, depression, or anxiety.

Research Proposal

I proposed a research project Mending Perceptions at the University of Cambridge which investigated the future architecture of psychiatric healthcare with a model which addresses stigma, therapeutic design, and a broader involvement in recovery. Through these conversations, my research interests shifted to the broader scale and how treatment might become more easily accessible and viable digitally. As such, my research interests are focused on researching existing digital well-being services and platforms.
